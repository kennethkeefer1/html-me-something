    • Hilt

    The style and structure of a lightsaber hilt often shows off the owner's personality as much as it does follow its function and usage. These are the common variants of lightsaber hilts.

    Standard

    The single standard hilt is the most frequently used of all hilt variants. It is able to adapt to most styles of lightsaber combat. Obi-Wan Kenobi and Luke Skywalker are examples of standard hilt users.

    Shoto

    For those looking for a lightsaber more relative to a diminutive size, the shoto style lightsaber is useful. It also is a handy compliment to a primary standard lightsaber for those wishing for the ability to dual wield with the Jar'Kai style. Yoda is a well known user.

    Dual Blade

    Twice the blades is twice the attack for those looking to wield a double bladed lightsaber. With proper training on this weapon, it is as dangerous to the wielder as to an opponent. Used by Darth Maul.

    Curved Hilt

    Allowing its wielder added precision and speed in attack, the curved hilt fits best for those that respect the dance of a dual more than the violence. Choice of former Jedi turned Sith, Count Dooku.

    • Color

    The color of a lightsaber comes from the single or multiple crystals inside the hilt that function to focus the plasma energy emission. Crystals can be natural or synthetic, as is often the choice of Sith lighsaber users. This is an overview of the most common lightsaber colors.

    Blue

    The lightsaber color associated withf Jedi Guardians, blue is the most common color of Jedi lightsaber. As Guardians are active in battle, blue lightsabers are often in action.

    Green

    Green is the second most common color of Jedi lightsabers. It is often related to Jedi Consulars. Focusing on peace, their green lightsabers are often Jedi Consulars' last resort.

    Purple

    A rare lightsaber color resulting from an amethyst crystal, purple was the choice of noted Jedi swordsman, Mace Windu.

    Red

    The traditional lightsaber color for the Sith, red is the byproduct of synthetic crystal usage.
